Barrel shipping FAQ

Plain-English answers to the questions UK senders ask most. Updated as customs rules and supplier policies change.

The basics

What is a "barrel" in shipping terms?

A 55-gallon (or 45-gallon) plastic or steel drum, sealed with a lid and shipped by sea freight. It's the standard unit the diaspora uses to send goods home, especially to the Caribbean and West Africa. Suppliers usually sell empty barrels for £20–£40.

What size barrel should I send?

The standard is a 55-gallon plastic drum: roughly 88cm tall and 58cm in diameter. 45-gallon steel drums are also common but heavier. Some shippers also offer crates or boxes if you don't need a full barrel.

How long does shipping take?

By sea freight (the standard for barrels):

  • Jamaica: ~3 weeks
  • Trinidad & Tobago: ~3–4 weeks
  • Guyana: ~4–5 weeks
  • (): ~3–4 weeks
  • (): ~4 weeks

Add 1–2 weeks for customs clearance and final delivery on the receiving end. Christmas-period shipments take longer due to volume — book early.

What does "door-to-door" actually include?

True door-to-door means: the shipper collects the barrel from your UK home, handles all freight and customs, and delivers it to a named address in the destination country. The recipient pays nothing on arrival.

Watch for "door-to-door" prices that exclude customs duty — that's a hidden cost the recipient ends up paying. Always ask: is duty included in the price?

What you can & can't send

What can I put in a barrel?

Generally allowed across most destinations:

  • Clothing and shoes
  • Non-perishable food: rice, tinned goods, dry goods, biscuits
  • Toiletries and personal care
  • Household items: bedding, towels, kitchenware
  • Baby goods and school supplies
  • Toys and gifts

Always confirm with your supplier — country rules differ.

What's banned or restricted?

Common restrictions:

  • Alcohol (heavily restricted or banned in most cases)
  • Fresh produce, meat, dairy
  • Weapons, ammunition, fireworks
  • Prescription medication (without paperwork)
  • High-value electronics (often subject to heavy duty)
  • Counterfeit goods
  • Anything illegal in the destination country

Specific bans vary. , for example, has a long restricted-imports list including used clothing in some categories. Always check with the supplier and check destination customs guidance.

Do I need to declare contents?

Yes. You'll need to provide a contents list (manifest) for customs. Be honest — under-declaring is risky and can result in seizure or fines on the recipient's end.

Can I send used clothes?

Generally yes for personal use to the Caribbean. has restrictions on commercial-quantity used clothing imports. If you're sending more than a few barrels of used clothing, ask the supplier specifically.

Cost & payment

What does it cost to ship a barrel?

Prices change with fuel, season and destination. We don't list "from" prices because they mislead — what you actually pay depends on barrel size, weight, collection location and whether customs is included. Compare verified suppliers for current pricing, or request a custom quote.

Who pays customs duty?

It depends on the service:

  • Door-to-door (all-inclusive): the UK shipper handles duty and bills it to you upfront.
  • Drop-off / port-to-port: the recipient usually pays duty when collecting from the destination port.

Always ask: "Is customs duty included?" before booking.

Are there hidden fees I should watch for?

Yes — common hidden charges:

  • Customs duty (if not included)
  • Port handling and delivery on the receiving end
  • "Storage" fees if collection is delayed
  • Fuel surcharges added at booking
  • Empty barrel cost (if you didn't supply your own)

A reputable shipper will give you a single all-in price. If they won't, ask why.

Insurance & what if it goes wrong

Is my barrel insured?

By default, most shippers offer only basic carrier liability — which is far below the value of typical contents. For full protection you usually need to declare a value and pay for additional cargo insurance. Ask your supplier specifically what's covered and what isn't.

What if my barrel is lost or damaged?

You should be entitled to compensation under the carrier's terms — but the amount depends on the contract and any insurance you took out. Keep:

  • A copy of your manifest (contents list)
  • Receipts for high-value items
  • Photos of the packed barrel before sealing
  • Your shipping receipt and tracking reference

If a shipper refuses to engage with a legitimate claim, you can share your experience with the community here.

How do I avoid getting scammed?

Red flags:

  • No physical UK address
  • Pressure to pay by bank transfer to a personal account
  • No tracking or paperwork
  • Prices much lower than the rest of the market
  • Refusal to give a written quote
  • Online reviews that all sound the same

Stick to verified suppliers, get everything in writing, and pay by card or via a method that gives you recourse if things go wrong.
